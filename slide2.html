  • Inadequate intake of calcium may not show obvious symptoms in the short-term because the human body maintains calcium levels in the blood by taking it from bone.
  • Over a long period, calcium intake below the recommended level can lead to low bone mass (osteopenia) and increase the risks of osteoporosis and bone fractures.
  • Symptoms of severe calcium deficiency include numbness and tingling in the fingers, convulsions and abnormal heart rhythms.

Source: NIH. Calcium: Fact sheet for consumers. Available at: http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/Calcium-QuickFacts/#h6
